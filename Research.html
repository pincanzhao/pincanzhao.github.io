Research Interests

My research interests span the intersection of intelligent transportation systems, artificial intelligence, and security, focusing on developing innovative solutions for next-generation transportation. Current research directions emphasize efficient and secure cooperative perception frameworks for connected and autonomous vehicles (CAVs), leveraging advanced machine learning techniques and privacy-preserving mechanisms.

Research & Project Experience

Postdoctoral Researcher — Intelligent Cooperative Perception for CAVs (Dec. 2025 - Present)

Western University, Dept. of Electrical and Computer Engineering, London, Canada. Advisor: Prof. Kelly Yili Tang

  • Designing a unified V2V-V2I cooperative perception framework that resolves data alignment across heterogeneous sensor modalities through spatiotemporal calibration and multi-level transformer-based fusion, directly addressing the coverage-reliability gap in single-paradigm systems.
  • Developing energy-aware edge intelligence for resource allocation in cooperative sensing networks, targeting 20–25% energy reduction via context-driven, hierarchical computation offloading without degrading safety-critical perception performance.
  • Investigating transformer architectures with domain-specific attention mechanisms for semantic traffic understanding, enabling proactive hazard anticipation beyond conventional object detection; federated learning is incorporated to achieve collaborative model training under strict data privacy constraints.
  • Research funded by the Western Postdoctoral Fellowship Award (2025–2027); targeting publications in IEEE TITS, TVT, TMC, and TON.

Research Assistant — ISAC and Cooperative Sensing for Vehicular Networks (Jan. 2023 - Sep. 2025)

Carleton University, Dept. of Systems and Computer Engineering, Ottawa, Canada

  • Formulated a 3D roadside sensor deployment optimization problem under coverage and cost constraints, and proposed a deployment strategy that increased sensing coverage by 30% over baseline grid-based layouts; results published in IEEE TITS and recognized with Best Paper Award at DIVANet’23.
  • Reformulated cooperative sensing scheduling as an offline reinforcement learning problem and developed a rewardless RL method that eliminates the need for hand-crafted reward functions, achieving robust sensor selection and resource allocation under dynamic traffic conditions; published in IEEE TVT.
  • Proposed an Improved Decision Transformer for cooperative sensing, integrating multi-head attention with sequential decision modeling to jointly optimize perception quality and communication overhead in real time; published in IEEE IoT Journal.

Research Lead — Privacy-Preserving Secure Data Sharing in Vehicular Networks (Jan. 2022 - Jan. 2023)

Xidian University, School of Telecommunication Engineering, Xi’an, China

  • Identified a fundamental trust-manageability trade-off in fully decentralized vehicular crowdsensing and proposed a Conditional Decentralized Architecture that combines on-chain smart contracts for auditability with off-chain control for operational flexibility, achieving effective privacy protection without sacrificing system governance.
  • Designed smart contract-based reputation protocols using zero-knowledge proofs to enable quality-aware contributor selection without exposing raw sensing data; demonstrated 96% malicious behavior detection accuracy in simulations.
  • Outcomes included a publication in IEEE TITS (2022), one granted China Patent on ZKP-based privacy protection (CN114760067B), and two filed patent applications on sensor deployment and data transmission decisions.

Research Assistant — Key Technologies for 6G Vehicular Services (Jan. 2021 - Dec. 2024)

Xidian University, School of Telecommunication Engineering, Xi’an, China

  • Modeled the sensing contribution incentive problem under strategic agent behavior as a Stackelberg supervision game and derived analytical equilibrium conditions; the resulting incentive mechanism provably discourages free-riding and improves sensing data quality, yielding a Best Paper Award at FCN’24 and a publication in IEEE TITS.
  • Developed a federated learning framework for CAVs incorporating cost-heterogeneity-aware model selection and gradient compression, reducing communication overhead while sustaining convergence accuracy across distributed vehicle nodes; published in IEEE TITS.
  • Designed blockchain-based vehicular sensing-sharing protocols with integrated privacy-preserving computation, improving system throughput by 60% over centralized baselines while maintaining data integrity; co-authored 3 IEEE TITS papers within this project.

Research Assistant — Integration of Communication and Computing in IoV (Sep. 2019 - Dec. 2022)

Xidian University, School of Telecommunication Engineering, Xi’an, China

  • Pioneered the application of blockchain-based decentralized crowdsensing to vehicular networks, developing a full-stack system (smart contracts, consensus protocol, incentive layer) that achieved 60% throughput improvement and 96% malicious node detection accuracy over centralized architectures.
  • Constructed a zero-knowledge proof and smart contract co-design for privacy-preserving information exchange, enabling verifiable data authenticity without revealing sensitive vehicle trajectories or identities.
  • Proposed targeted information dissemination algorithms for emergency scenarios that jointly optimize routing and content relevance, ensuring timely delivery to at-risk vehicles; published in IEEE TITS and IEEE VTC 2020-Fall.
