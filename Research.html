Research Vision

My research lets connected vehicles see beyond their own line of sight — fusing perception across many vehicles and roadside sensors into one shared, trustworthy view of the road, efficiently and privately.

My work spans the intersection of intelligent transportation systems, artificial intelligence, and security, building efficient and secure cooperative perception frameworks for connected and autonomous vehicles (CAVs) by advancing machine learning techniques and privacy-preserving mechanisms for next-generation transportation. Research Contributions

Cooperative Perception & V2X

  • A unified V2V–V2I cooperative perception framework — spatiotemporal calibration and multi-level transformer-based fusion across heterogeneous sensors, with energy-aware edge offloading. OngoingWestern Fellowship
  • 3D roadside sensor deployment optimized under coverage and cost constraints — 30% higher sensing coverage than grid-based baselines. IEEE TITSDIVANet’23 Best Paper
  • Integrated sensing-and-communication (ISAC) and real-time cooperative sensing scheduling for vehicular networks. IEEE IoT-J

Learning for Autonomous Decisions

  • Rewardless offline reinforcement learning for cooperative sensing — robust sensor selection and resource allocation without hand-crafted reward functions. IEEE TVT
  • An Improved Decision Transformer coupling multi-head attention with sequential decision modeling to jointly optimize perception quality and communication overhead in real time. IEEE IoT-J
  • Communication-efficient federated learning for CAVs — cost-heterogeneity-aware model selection and gradient compression that cut communication while sustaining convergence accuracy. IEEE TITS

Trustworthy & Private Sharing

  • A game-theoretic supervision incentive mechanism that provably discourages free-riding and raises sensing-data quality. IEEE TITSFCN’24 Best Paper
  • A Conditional Decentralized crowdsensing architecture (on-chain auditability with off-chain control) — 60% higher throughput and 96% malicious-node detection over centralized baselines. IEEE TITS
  • Privacy-preserving cooperative sensing via encrypted spatial matching, private set intersection, and zero-knowledge proofs. IEEE IoT-JIEEE TCC
  • Post-quantum revocable ring signatures and edge-assisted batch authentication for vehicular cooperative perception. IEEE TMCIEEE ICC
